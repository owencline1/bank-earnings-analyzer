## The five staged questions, answered

1. **Did the NII guide-cut survive earnings week? It didn't just survive — TFC cut again.** FY26 NII growth guided down a second time, +2–3% → **+1–1.5%**, the same week JPM/BAC/PNC raised and WFC/C/USB printed expanding NII. The lone-cutter divergence is now the starkest line in the quarter's stance matrix. But the composition matters before calling it damning: Maguire attributed it to (a) deliberate runoff of low-return consumer portfolios (marine/RV exited, auto production cut ~40%, ~$7–8B less annual production) which lowers NII but *raises* ROTCE, (b) broad loan-spread compression (-5 to -10 bps YoY expected), and (c) deposit mix shifting toward higher-rate products (DDA ~27% → ~25% by year-end).
   > "We now expect net interest income to increase approximately 1 to 1.5%. Our updated outlook reflects actions we have taken to improve profitability as well as certain market dynamics. First, we are continuing to optimize less strategic and lower return lending portfolios that offer limited relationship potential which has the effect of reducing NII and net interest margin but improves ROTCE." — Michael Maguire, CFO
   My read: part honest structural admission (spread compression and rate-seeking deposits are industry-wide; peers absorbed them with bigger fee engines), part confirmation that the standalone balance sheet isn't capturing what peers capture. The ROTCE print (~15%, up from 13.8%) says the optimization trade is at least doing what management claims — this quarter.
2. **Buyback delivery: yes, on the number.** $1.2B repurchased, exactly as promised in April; $5B FY target intact; >100% of earnings returned; CET1 still rose 10 bps to 10.9%. The M&A-target rebuttal was delivered — and the market question showed up anyway, just wearing a different suit (see 5).
3. **Credit: the 61 bps sore spot healed.** NCOs fell 11 bps to 50, FY guide held at 55, provision ($395M) ran below charge-offs ($414M). The indirect-auto nonaccrual reclass added 1 bp to NPLs and management again flagged it as technical, not deterioration. **CRE stance flipped flat → releasing** — ALLL down 2 bps on resolved commercial/CRE problem credits and improving office/multifamily. That puts TFC in the release camp with BAC, WFC, PNC (USB the lone "flat"; JPM and C silent) — the 3-way CRE split the what-to-watch worried about didn't materialize.
4. **Deposit costs: competition bit, as management themselves warned.** Q1's best-in-group -14 bps trend reversed to +1 bp (IB 2.10%, total 1.56%). The real story is forward-looking: the DDA remix to ~25% is cited as the single largest headwind inside the NII cut. TFC now sits in the "rising" camp with USB, against BAC/WFC "falling" and JPM/PNC "peaking."
5. **Loan growth: soft again, and deliberately so.** +0.7% LQ repeats Q1; commercial +1.3% LQ (C&I ~+8% YoY) is real but the consumer runoff nets it to the weakest growth stance of the seven ("soft" vs four "accelerating"). And the underperformance question came as predicted — not Najarian on M&A math this time, but Mike Mayo calling the stock "dead money" for a decade, with Rogers pointing to technology, talent, and **incoming CEO Mike Lyons as an accelerant** rather than offering new commitments.

## Did the thesis change vs Q1?

**Sharpened, not changed.** TFC is running the opposite play from every peer: shrink low-return assets, cut the NII guide, buy back stock at maximum pace, and point to ROTCE. Q2 delivered the proof points management controls (NCOs down, ROTCE ~15%, buyback on schedule, CET1 up) while conceding the ones it doesn't (NII, spreads, deposit mix). The bear case is now cleaner too: if the C&I wave keeps lifting peers' NII while TFC's optimization anniversaries, FY27 needs the fee/efficiency story to carry — with a new CEO in the seat.

## Cross-read hook (the full seven, live Q2 data)

- **NII/NIM: 6 up (JPM/BAC/PNC raised; WFC/C/USB expanding), TFC alone down — twice in a row.**
- **CRE reserves: 4 releasing (BAC, WFC, PNC, TFC), USB flat, JPM/C silent.** The reserve-build worry that framed the quarter's setup is dead on live data — nobody built.
- **Deposit costs: genuine 3-way split** — falling (BAC, WFC) vs peaking (JPM, PNC) vs rising (USB, TFC), C mixed. This is now the most contested real dimension of the quarter.
- **Capital: 5 increasing, JPM/BAC steady** — with the philosophical bookends on the same dimension: Dimon rejecting "buybacks = returning money" at ~3x tangible book vs TFC returning >100% of earnings at a fraction of that multiple.
