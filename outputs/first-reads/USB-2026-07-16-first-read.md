## What they said (call)

- **Guidance raised hard:** 2026 revenue outlook now **7%–9% growth, up from 4%–6%** (5%–7% ex-BTIG). Q3 NII guided +4%–6% YoY; Q3 fee growth 12%–14% incl. BTIG.
- **BTIG is the story management wants told:** ~$98M revenue in its first month ("strongest monthly revenue performance in BTIG's history"), ~$200M/quarter expected in 2H26, ~15% contribution margin, ~$60M merger costs in 2026. Management says no further capital-markets bolt-on is needed to hit the >10%-of-revenue capital-markets goal.
- **Amazon Small Business Portfolio acquisition** closing mid-August brings ~**$160M of reserve build** — a flagged, one-time credit item for Q3, not deterioration.
- **Buybacks flat at $200M**, same as Q1. Management "intends to increase buybacks as the company approaches an adjusted CET1 level of about 10%."
- Merchant-processing softness attributed partly to Europe (post-war impacts, loss of some non-strategic distribution partners).

## Did the thesis change vs Q1?

**Thesis strengthened, with one stance flip.**

- Q1's open question was whether the NII/fee inflection was real. It was: NIM +2 bps QoQ again, record revenue, and a 3-point revenue-guide raise is the largest guide move of the seven banks so far this quarter.
- Efficiency (57.1% from 58.2%) and ROTCE (18.7% from 17.0%) both broke the right way — the perennial "USB lags on returns" knock is fading this quarter.
- **Stance flip — capital return: Q1 "increasing" → Q2 "steady/deferred, M&A-first."** Q1's signal was buybacks gliding up ($100M→$200M). Instead buybacks held at $200M and capital went to acquisitions (BTIG closed, Amazon SBP pending). The Q1 Najarian question (Category II AOCI cliff constraining the 70–75% payout path) stays live — buyback acceleration is now explicitly conditioned on "adjusted CET1 ~10%."
- Credit: still clean and improving (NCO 0.53%, NPA 0.33%). ACL ratio fell to 1.94% from 2.00%, but that's loan-growth denominator, not a release — provision matched charge-offs.

## Cross-read hook (vs JPM / BAC / PNC, processed Jul 14–15)

- **NII/NIM raised: now 4-for-4.** USB joins JPM, BAC, PNC in raising — and USB's raise is the biggest.
- **Capital return is the live divergence:** PNC increasing (18% dividend hike), JPM/BAC steady with large buybacks, **USB the only one funneling capital into M&A instead of buybacks.** Who's right depends on whether BTIG/Amazon SBP out-earn a buyback at ~2x tangible book — management's $200M/quarter BTIG claim is checkable in Q3.
- **Deposit costs:** USB's improvement is mix-driven (record consumer deposits, Smartly balances >$84B) — closer to BAC's "falling via mix" camp than JPM/PNC's "peaking."
- **CRE:** no discrete CRE commentary in the release (JPM also silent; BAC/PNC releasing). Check the transcript when it posts.
