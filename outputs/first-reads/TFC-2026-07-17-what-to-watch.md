# TFC Q2 2026 — what to watch (pre-earnings)

**Written:** Thursday, July 16, 2026, ~12:50 PM ET — ahead of Truist's print Friday, July 17, premarket (call typically 8:00 AM ET). Last of the seven banks to report; the other six are in hand, so tomorrow's read completes the quarter.

## Where TFC left off (Q1 2026 baseline, from the analyzer)

- **NII/NIM: the only one of our seven that CUT.** FY26 NII guide lowered to 2%–3% growth (from 3%–4%) after removing two assumed Fed cuts; NIM fell 5 bps QoQ to 3.02%; Q1 NII -2.8% QoQ.
- Credit: NCOs 61 bps (+4 bps QoQ) — highest of the seven — though the NPL uptick was a nonaccrual-criteria change on indirect auto, not deterioration. NDFI portfolio disclosed at 12% of loans.
- Deposit costs: falling (interest-bearing cost -14 bps QoQ to 2.09%) but management flagged competitive pressure and yield-seeking clients.
- Capital return: aggressive — $1.1B bought back in Q1, FY guide raised to $5B (from $4B), **$1.2B targeted for Q2**. CET1 10.8%, target ~10% by 2027.
- Q&A sore spot: Erika Najarian pressed management on the Bloomberg piece framing TFC as an M&A target (low market-cap-to-core-deposits). Management was defensive.
- ROTCE 13.8% — the lowest of the seven.

## The five questions for tomorrow

1. **Does the NII guide-cut survive earnings week?** All six peers that have printed raised or beat on NII (JPM, BAC, PNC, USB raised guides; WFC held ~$50B; C beat big). TFC cut in April on "higher for longer." If TFC holds 2%–3% while everyone else raises, the divergence is stark: either TFC's rate view is the honest one, or its balance sheet just isn't capturing what peers are capturing. This is the headline cross-read question of the day.
2. **Buyback delivery: did the promised ~$1.2B show up?** TFC guided the most aggressive capital return of the seven relative to size. Delivery matters double given the M&A-target narrative — a full-pace buyback is management's rebuttal. Compare against USB's opposite posture (buybacks flat, capital to M&A).
3. **NCOs vs 61 bps, and the indirect-auto noise.** Does the auto nonaccrual reclass wash out? Any CRE reserve action — TFC was "flat" on CRE in Q1 while BAC/PNC are now releasing. A TFC build against peers releasing would make the CRE cross-read 3-way.
4. **Deposit costs: still falling, or did competition bite?** Q1's -14 bps was the best trend in the group, but management themselves flagged yield-seeking pressure. Watch total deposit cost vs 1.55% and any beta commentary.
5. **Loan growth vs the group's C&I surge.** Peers reported broad C&I strength (PNC +4% LQ, BAC ninth straight growth quarter, USB +3.0% LQ). TFC guided only "steady" (+0.7% LQ in Q1). If TFC misses the industry-wide C&I wave, expect analysts to ask why — and expect the M&A question again if the standalone growth story looks thin.
